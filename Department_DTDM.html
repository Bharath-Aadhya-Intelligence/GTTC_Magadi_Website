About the Department

Tool and Die Making is a specialized branch of manufacturing engineering that involves designing, fabricating, and maintaining tools, dies, molds, and special equipment used in various manufacturing processes. These tools are critical for mass production, ensuring precision, efficiency, and consistency across industries like automotive, aerospace, electronics, and consumer goods.


The Diploma in Tool and Die Making (DTDM) at GTTC Magadi is a technical education program typically spanning 4 years (including apprenticeship), aimed at equipping students with the necessary skills and knowledge to excel in the industry. The curriculum combines theoretical learning with practical training on advanced machinery.

Infrastructure & Laboratories

The DTDM department is equipped with industry-standard facilities to ensure hands-on learning:


  • CNC Machining Lab: Featuring advanced milling and turning centers.
  • Conventional Machine Shop: Lathees, milling machines, and surface grinders.
  • CAD/CAM Center: Modern software for tool and mold design.
  • Quality & Measurement Lab: Precision instruments for tool verification.

About GTTC

Government Tool Room and Training Centre (GTTC) was established in 1972 with the participation of the Karnataka State Government and the Government of Denmark. It provides high-quality technical education and skill development.
